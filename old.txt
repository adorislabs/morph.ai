## SLIDE 2 — Problem Statement
**The web is a one-size-fits-all disaster.
**
Think about the last ad you clicked. It was targeted, specific, promised you something
relevant. Then you landed on a page — and it had no idea who you were or why you came.
Same page for everyone. The ad knew you; the page didn't.
This is the problem the web has had since the beginning. And the numbers show it:
- Average bounce rate on landing pages: **55–70%**
- Global ad spend wasted on poor landing page experiences: **$100B+ annually**
- Personalization today means: "Change the headline based on which ad you clicked.
" That's
it.
But the problem is getting worse, not better. By 2027,
**~30% of web traffic will be AI
agents**
— personal shopping bots, research assistants, price comparison tools. They don't
read pages. They parse data. Today's web has no idea what to do with them.
The web needs to do more than swap a headline. It needs to *think*
.
---
## SLIDE 3 — Proposed Solution
**morph.ai turns every URL into a living, sensing agent.
**
We started with the best ideas in agentic web personalization — real-time intent detection,
AI-driven content personalization, human-in-the-loop controls — and asked: what does the
*next generation* of this look like?
Our answer is a platform with three layers:
**Layer 1 — It already does everything you'd expect from an agentic web platform:**
Ads personalization. Journey tracking. Audience-level tailoring. LLM-based personalization
for visitors from ChatGPT or Gemini. A/B experimentation. CDP-synced segments. Brand
guardrails. Enterprise compliance. Drop-in JS SDK. This is the foundation.
**Layer 2 — Then we go inside the user's head:**
morph reads *cursor body language*
— micro-hesitations, scroll velocity, hover patterns —
to classify the visitor's actual emotional state: confused, skeptical, or ready to convert. The
page adapts to that state, not just to their audience segment. We call it the Kinetic Intent
Engine. No other platform does this.
**Layer 3 — And then we go beyond the human:**
When an AI agent visits (a shopping bot, a research assistant, a price comparison tool),
morph detects it and opens a machine-to-machine channel. Instead of the bot parsing your
HTML, it gets a direct JSON-RPC handshake — pricing, features, layout — negotiated
programmatically. Your website talks to their AI. We built the infrastructure for a web that's
half-human, half-machine.
All of this happens at the edge, in under 200ms, with zero layout shift.

-
**Synthetic Simulator (Pre-Flight)**
— Before traffic hits, 5,000 LLM-driven synthetic

-
**Edge Mutation Layer**
— Instead of client-side JS swapping components after load,
morph rebuilds the DOM at the Cloudflare edge (Rust + WebAssembly) before the first byte
reaches the browser. True zero-flicker. Sub-200ms. The page is built for you before your
browser asks for it.
-
**Cross-Agent Negotiation Protocol**
— Detects AI agent visitors and opens a JSON-RPC
2.0 channel over HTTP/3. The agent negotiates pricing, feature visibility, and content depth
directly. Structured response, no HTML parsing needed. The first web layer built for the
machine-to-machine economy.

## SLIDE 6 — Workflow Diagram
**The morph.ai flow — start to finish:**
*Real-time path:*
Someone clicks an ad. The 8KB SDK loads. It starts watching: cursor movement, scroll
speed, hesitation zones, User-Agent string. The Kinetic Intent Engine classifies in real-time
— what is this person's state of mind?
If it's a human: the Edge Mutation Layer takes the intent classification + pre-flight
optimizations and builds a custom DOM at the CDN edge. The page renders in under
200ms. Zero flickering. As they keep browsing, the system keeps reading and keeps
adapting. It's a loop.
If it's an AI agent: the Cross-Agent Negotiation Protocol activates. JSON-RPC handshake.
Bot queries what it needs. morph responds with structured data — pricing, features, content
depth. No HTML, no guessing.
*Pre-flight path (async, before any traffic):*
Brand adds a URL. The Synthetic Simulator spins up 5,000 LLM-driven personas. They
browse. The system finds friction. The page is pre-optimized. By the time real visitors show
up, the page is already crash-tested.
